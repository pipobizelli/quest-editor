# HeroQuest — Core Rules

> Reference document for the quest-editor LLM agents (narrator, strategist, etc.).
> Based on the original HeroQuest Instruction Booklet (Milton Bradley / Games Workshop, 1989-1990).

---

## Overview

HeroQuest is a fantasy adventure board game set in an underground stone labyrinth controlled by the Evil Sorcerer Zargon and his forces of Chaos. Four Heroes — Barbarian, Dwarf, Elf, and Wizard — work together to complete Quests, defeat monsters, and find treasure.

---

## Heroes — Starting Stats

| Hero      | Movement | Attack Dice | Defend Dice | Body Points | Mind Points |
|-----------|----------|-------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Barbarian | 2d6      | 3           | 2           | 8           | 2           |
| Dwarf     | 2d6      | 2           | 2           | 7           | 3           |
| Elf       | 2d6      | 2           | 2           | 6           | 4           |
| Wizard    | 2d6      | 1           | 2           | 4           | 6           |

### Starting Equipment

- **Barbarian**: Broadsword (3 Attack Dice)
- **Dwarf**: Shortsword (2 Attack Dice)
- **Elf**: Shortsword (2 Attack Dice) — also has spells (1 spell group, 3 spells)
- **Wizard**: Dagger (1 Attack Die) — primary spellcaster (3 spell groups, 9 spells)

### Stat Descriptions

- **Body Points**: Physical strength / hit points. When reduced to zero, the Hero dies.
- **Mind Points**: Wisdom and resistance to magic. Higher Mind = more resistant to spells.
- **Attack Dice**: Number of white combat dice rolled when attacking (determined by weapon).
- **Defend Dice**: Number of white combat dice rolled when defending (base 2, modified by armor).

---

## Turn Order

1. Zargon reads the Parchment Text aloud at the start of the Quest.
2. Play begins with the Hero seated to Zargon's left and continues clockwise.
3. After ALL Heroes have completed their turns, it is Zargon's turn (moves all monsters).
4. This sequence continues until the Quest is achieved or all Heroes leave/die.

---

## On a Hero's Turn

A Hero may **move** and then **perform an action**, OR **perform an action** and then **move**. You may also choose to only move or only perform an action.

### Movement

- Roll 2 red dice (2d6) to determine movement distance in squares.
- Move square by square along corridors and into rooms.
- You do **NOT** have to move the full distance indicated by the dice.
- **Cannot** move diagonally.
- **Cannot** pass over monsters, move through walls, or pass through closed doors.
- **Can** pass through squares occupied by other Heroes.
- **Exception**: You MAY share a square with another Hero when entering through a door. You may NOT stop on an occupied square otherwise.
- Corridors are 1 square wide; rooms are enclosed by gray lines.

### The 6 Hero Actions

A Hero may perform **any one** of the following actions per turn:

#### Action 1 — Attack

- You may attack any monster **adjacent** to you (horizontally or vertically).
- Some weapons allow **diagonal** attacks (longsword, halberd, greatsword, staff, spear).
- Special ranged weapons (longbow, crossbow) can attack any visible monster but **NOT** adjacent ones.
- You may only attack **one** monster per turn with **one** weapon.
- The Elf can attack diagonally only when adjacent to the monster from one of the four diagonal positions.

#### Action 2 — Cast a Spell (Elf and Wizard only)

- Must have Line of Sight to the target (see Line of Sight rules below).
- A spell may be cast on yourself, another Hero, or a monster.
- Each spell can only be cast **once per Quest**. Once cast, the spell card is discarded for the remainder of that Quest.
- A spell may be cast **instead of** attacking. You may only cast one spell per turn.
- **Important**: If you cast a spell and have not already performed an action, you may NOT perform another action that turn. You **may** cast a healing spell on yourself after being attacked on your turn.

#### Action 3 — Search for Treasure

- Can only search in **rooms**, not corridors.
- A room may only be searched by all 4 Heroes, but each individual Hero may search only once on their own turn.
- Must verbally declare: "I am searching for treasure."
- If Zargon has nothing special (from Quest Notes), the Hero draws a random Treasure Card.
- **Wandering Monsters**: About half of treasure cards contain Wandering Monsters. The Hero must fight immediately.
- A room with a monster **cannot** be searched. Must kill all monsters first.
- **Do not move** your Hero figure when you search.

#### Action 4 — Search for Secret Doors

- Must verbally declare: "I am searching for secret doors."
- You can only search for secret doors if there are **no monsters visible** to you.
- Zargon will place a secret door tile on the game board if one is located in the room or corridor.
- The door is not considered **OPEN** until you move adjacent to it and declare you are opening it.

#### Action 5 — Search for Traps

- Must verbally declare: "I am searching for traps."
- You can only search for traps in the **room** you are in.
- If a trap is discovered, a Hero may be able to JUMP or DISARM it.
- Searching for traps does **not** spring the trap.

#### Action 6 — Disarm a Trap

- You need a **Tool Kit** (purchased from the Armory) to disarm a trap.
- Announce your intent, move onto the trap square, and roll 1 combat die.
- **Skull** = trap is disarmed and removed from the board.
- **Black Shield** = trap springs, but you suffer no damage (trap disarmed).
- **White Shield** = trap springs with body damage.
- A disarmed trap is considered "gone" and is not put on the gameboard.
- **Note**: The Dwarf does not need a Tool Kit to disarm traps (innate ability). This applies only in the base game, not Quest Packs.

## Combat

### How a Hero Attacks

1. Roll the number of **white combat dice** equal to your weapon's Attack strength.
2. Each **Skull** rolled is a hit.
3. The defending monster then rolls its Defend Dice.
4. Each **Shield** (black shield for monsters) rolled blocks 1 hit.
5. Each unblocked hit = 1 Body Point of damage.
6. If the monster's Body Points reach zero, it is dead and removed from the board.

### How a Hero Defends

1. Roll the number of **white combat dice** equal to your Defend value (base 2, modified by armor).
2. Each **White Shield** rolled blocks 1 hit.
3. Unblocked hits = Body Point damage. Record on Character Sheet.

### How a Monster Attacks

1. The monster rolls its **Attack Dice** (number shown on Monster Card).
2. Each **Skull** = a hit.
3. The Hero defends by rolling their Defend Dice.
4. Each **White Shield** = blocks 1 hit.
5. A monster may attack only **one** Hero per turn, only if adjacent.
6. A monster's attack strength is based on its natural abilities and does **not** depend on a weapon.

### How a Monster Defends

1. Roll the number of **Defend Dice** shown on the Monster Card.
2. Each **Black Shield** rolled blocks 1 hit.

### Combat Dice Faces

Each white combat die has:
- 3 Skulls (hit)
- 2 White Shields (Hero defense)
- 1 Black Shield (Monster defense)

### Diagonal Combat

- Weapons that allow diagonal attacks: **Longsword**, **Halberd**, **Greatsword**, **Staff**, **Spear**.
- The Wizard with a staff is in a "safe" position when attacking diagonally (monster cannot counter-attack if not adjacent).
- Both the Wizard and the Barbarian may attack through a doorway diagonally.

## Line of Sight

- Draw an invisible straight line from the center of the caster's square to the center of the target's square.
- If the line does **not** cross a wall, closed door, or the corner of a wall, the target is visible.
- If the line just touches a corner or even slightly crosses a wall edge, the target is **not** visible.
- Open doors do **not** block line of sight.
- Figures (Heroes and monsters) do **not** block line of sight.

---

## Doors

- All doors start **closed**. Once opened, a door can never be closed again.
- A Hero opens a door by moving adjacent to it and declaring they open it.
- When a Hero opens a door, Zargon will open the door piece and reveal the room. Zargon then places the contents (monsters, furniture, traps) according to the Quest Notes.
- Opening a door, "looking" and replacing it with a closed door are **all considered actions** — simply opening counts as part of movement, not as an action itself.
- **Note**: Opening a door is NOT one of the 6 actions. It is done as part of movement.

---

## Blocked Square Tiles

- Zargon places a blocked square tile on the gameboard as soon as a square tile becomes visible to the Hero.
- These tiles show where extra walls have been built.
- Neither Heroes nor monsters can move through blocked squares.

## Traps

### The 4 Kinds of Traps

Trap locations are marked in gold on the Quest Maps. Monsters do **not** spring hidden traps.

#### 1. Pit Traps

- A covered hole in the floor, found during a search.
- If discovered before stepping on it, a Hero can JUMP or DISARM it.
- If a Hero moves onto a pit trap square (unaware), Zargon says "You have just stumbled into a pit!" and places the pit tile.
- **In a pit**: You may attack and defend, but your minimum attack/defend is always 1 combat die, even if the pit would reduce it to zero. This applies to monsters as well.
- Normally, you can move out of a pit on your next turn.
- Once sprung and a Hero placed, the trap cannot be disarmed or jumped — move to escape.
- A sprung pit is a **permanent** block in the game. A pit in the corner has only 1 space open to jump across.

#### 2. Falling Block Traps

- Zargon gives you 3 combat dice to roll.
- **Skull** = 1 Body Point of damage. You may NOT defend.
- After the trap springs, you must decide: move to an empty space ahead or move back.
- The space is now a **permanent** block. It cannot be disarmed or jumped.

#### 3. Spear Traps

- Found during a search or when moving onto the square.
- When sprung, you roll 1 combat die:
  - **Skull** = 1 Body Point of damage (ends your turn).
  - **Black or White Shield** = dodged, no damage. You may continue moving. The spear trap is now gone forever and the square can be moved onto safely.

#### 4. Chest/Furniture Traps

- Can be poisonous gas, explosive latch, or a poison needle.
- Found when searching a room for treasure.
- Once sprung, the chest/furniture looks dangerous. A Hero may attempt to DISARM it on their next turn.

### Jumping a Trap

- Must have at least 2 squares of movement remaining.
- Must move onto the square ADJACENT to the trap and attempt to jump.
- Roll 1 combat die:
  - **Skull** = you spring the trap and suffer its corresponding damage.
  - **White/Black Shield** = you successfully jump to the square beyond the trap.
- If the path is blocked by a monster, you fall into the pit (if pit trap).
- Monsters and a swarm can always successfully jump over a pit.
- Heroes must jump across — from adjacent to the square beyond.

## Wandering Monsters

- When a Hero draws a Wandering Monster or Hazard card from the treasure deck, a monster appears.
- The Hero must fight immediately (roll combat dice, defend, etc.).
- As Zargon, you must place the monster and refer to the Monster Chart on the Information Screen for the correct monster type.
- If the surrounding squares are occupied, place the Wandering Monster as close as possible. If no placement is possible, the monster appears next to the Hero on Zargon's next turn.
- Wandering Monsters move and act like other monsters.

---

## Searching for Treasure

- **Treasure Cards**: These are "good" and "bad". Good ones give gold coins, potions, or items. Bad ones are Wandering Monsters or Hazards.
- Treasure cards must be **STANDING** (upright in deck) before a Hero draws one.
- **Important**: Treasure is found only in rooms, not corridors. A room may be searched by all 4 Heroes, but each Hero may do so only once on their own turn.
- Some treasures are special and described in the Quest Notes. These override random treasure cards.
- Gold coins found can be recorded on the Character Sheet and used between Quests at the Armory.

---

## Potions

- A Hero may drink a potion at **any time** during the Quest.
- The way a potion works and how long its effects last are described in the Quest Book.
- **Healing Potions**: Restore Body Points to starting value. May also give one to a fellow Hero.
- Potions are even more valuable if Healing Body Points are reduced to zero — you may immediately drink to save yourself.

---

## Death and Escaping Death

### What Happens When a Hero Dies

- Body Points reduced to zero = death.
- You are out of the game for that Quest.
- Your belongings (weapons, armor, treasure) remain in the room/corridor where you died. Any other Hero in that room can pick them up.
- If no other Heroes are in the room, the items are lost.

### Escaping Death

- If you have a **Healing Potion**, you can immediately drink it when Body Points reach zero, restoring your Body Points to starting value.
- A fellow Hero or the Elf/Wizard can cast a **Healing Spell** on you — but only if it is their turn and they have not already performed an action.
- **Important**: After casting a healing spell, the caster has used their action for that turn.

---

## Monster Movement

- Each monster may perform a **move** and an **action**, or an **action** and then **move** (same as Heroes).
- Monster movement is determined by the Movement value on the Monster Card (fixed number, not dice).
- Monsters move the **entire distance** indicated (no rolling).
- Unlike Heroes, monsters do **not** spring hidden traps, do **not** have to search for them, and do not need to disarm them.

### Monsters May:

- Move or attack diagonally
- Open or close doors
- Share a square on the gameboard
- Pass over Heroes (like Heroes pass through each other)

### Monsters May NOT:

- Search for treasure
- Search for secret doors
- Move through walls
- Move through blocked squares

### Monster Actions

Each monster may perform **one** of the following:
1. **ATTACK** — same as Hero attack but using monster stats
2. **CAST A CHAOS SPELL** — only certain monsters as noted in Quest Notes

---

## Zargon's Turn

- After all 4 Heroes have completed their turns, Zargon moves ALL monsters currently on the gameboard.
- Each monster may perform a move and then an action, or an action and then move.
- Monsters are moved according to Zargon's strategy — they can be moved in any order.
- Zargon should carefully watch the Heroes' movement and refer to the Quest Map and Quest Book.
- When a Hero "looks" down a corridor or through an open door, Zargon must place any monsters and blocked square tiles that are directly in line of sight.

---

## Between Quests

### The Armory

- After successfully completing a Quest, Heroes may visit the Armory.
- Body and Mind Points are automatically restored to starting values.
- All spells are restored.
- Heroes keep their gold coins, weapons, armor, and treasures from previous Quests.
- Gold coins accumulated can be spent on new equipment.
- See `heroquest-armory.md` for full item list and prices.

### Quest Rewards

- There is usually a final treasure or reward for completing a Quest.
- Circle the corresponding Quest number on your Character Sheet.
- Your reward efforts are split evenly among yourselves.

---

## Ending the Quest

- A Quest ends when the Heroes have achieved the Quest goal **AND** returned to the stairway.
- Quest may also end by all 4 Heroes dying, or players voluntarily returning to the stairway before completing the goal.
- The safety of early return is at the players' discretion.

---

## Key Rules Summary

1. Heroes move with 2d6; monsters move with fixed values.
2. One action per turn (attack, cast spell, search, or disarm).
3. Skulls = hits; White Shields = Hero blocks; Black Shields = Monster blocks.
4. Rooms must be cleared of monsters before searching.
5. Each spell may be cast only once per Quest.
6. Treasure is found only in rooms, not corridors.
7. Opening a door is part of movement, not an action.
8. Dead Heroes lose all items unless another Hero is in the same room.
9. Between Quests, Body/Mind/Spells are fully restored.
